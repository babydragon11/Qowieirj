4-H Club Projects

Explore our active hands-on youth programs centered around the historic livestock and fields of Tollgate Farms.

In 4-H, members choose specialized projects to work on throughout the year under guidance from adult volunteers. Our unique access to Tollgate Farms lets our club explore livestock, environment, and maker disciplines at an advanced level.

Animals: Dorper & Katahdin Hair Sheep

Small Ruminant Livestock Management

Members working on this project dive deep into Tollgate's historic pasture management systems. Because Tollgate focuses heavily on direct local-market production, members learn about livestock genetics and pasture rotation strategies.

  • Core Skills: Dynamic grazing management, animal nutrition balancing, and fleece assessment.
  • Fair Outcomes: Market lamb class, breeding ewe exhibition, and livestock showmanship.
Animals: Boer & Nigerian Dwarf Goats

Goat Husbandry & Animal Science

Goats are a major highlight of the Tollgate barns. This project handles both heavy market Boer meat goats and dairy-producing Nigerian Dwarf lines, teaching members animal health foundations on smaller, highly intelligent stock.

  • Core Skills: Biosecurity protocols, health diagnostics, and milk production mathematics.
  • Fair Outcomes: Dairy milk yield testing, market goat judging, and agility/showmanship classes.
Animals: Heritage Chickens, Turkeys & Ducks

Avian Science & Embryology

Perfect for members of all ages, our poultry track handles everything from backyard egg productions to heritage breed conservation. We run incubation programs inside Tollgate's classroom facilities.

  • Core Skills: Egg incubation engineering, biological biosecurity, and breed identification.
  • Fair Outcomes: Market broiler pens, egg production classes, and poultry showmanship.
Focus: Sustainable Crops & Soil Microbiology

Horticulture & Greenhouse Production

Utilizing the operational greenhouses and community garden plots at Tollgate Farms, members discover the complex world of plant biology, local ecosystem interactions, and commercial organic vegetable sales.

  • Core Skills: Composting chemistry, seedling management, and integrated pest management (IPM).
  • Fair Outcomes: Crop displays, heirloom vegetable collections, and landscape design notebooks.
